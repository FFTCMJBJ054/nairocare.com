About Us

We believe we are only as good as the good we do

Founded in 2019, Nairocare is a social enterprise that applies technology and innovative tools to raise funds and resources for Internally Displaced Persons(IDPs) in Africa. A pioneer in the field of social advocacy married with the use of financial technology tools. Nairocare uses an array of technology tools to simplify fundraising and crowdfunding for the Internally Displaced Persons(IDP) across Nigeria. We are in constant partnership with civil society, NGOs, CSOs, public institutions and the media, chiefly in the areas of advocacy, community engagement, research, innovation, and technology. Nairocare methodology is deploying refined skill sets to creatively develop tools and platforms to strategically crowdfund and raise fund for the education, shelter, health, and well-being of every Internally Displaced Persons(IDP)

Why Donate? Nigeria is plagued with several challenges, a few are illiteracy, unemployment, poverty, crime, terrorism, lack of basic infrastructure and many more. The Nigerian Government cannot solve these problems alone. They need privately owned businesses and citizens to partner with in transforming our country. Three of the biggest challenges of Nigeria are a lack of quality education, poor health care facilities and high rate of unemployment. An IDP (Internally Displaced Persons) web app provides an innovative and pragmatic solution to the problems and need of Internally displaced persons (IDPs) in Nigeria through technology. IDP web app is a technology platform aimed at alleviating the problem of illiteracy, hunger, poverty, and lack of shelter among the IDPs. The Displacement Tracking Matrix round XXI of January 2018 identified estimated 1.7 million IDPs in over 321,580 households across six states of North-East Nigeria with 40 per cent residing in camp-like settings in urban areas, plus 1.4 million returnees. This huge numbers indicate that the problem of IDPs needs urgent attention from both citizens and government. Nigeria has so much challenges that only government cannot solve. It requires a concerted effort from Government, Businesses and Citizens to overcome these challenges. Three of the biggest problem of Nigeria are Security, Education and Health. Nigeria has an estimated 1.7 million Internally Displaced Persons. Nigeria has the world largest number of out of School children at 13.2 Million. Malaria is a risk for 97% of Nigeria's population. The drivers of displacement in Nigeria are multi-faceted, complex and often overlapping. In the marginalised north-east, the emergence of the militant Islamist group, Boko Haram, has caused large numbers of displacements since 2014. In the Middle Belt region, competition between pastoralists and farmers has caused tensions, culminating in significant levels of violence and displacement. Flooding displaces thousands of people every year. In 2018, 541,000 new displacements were recorded in Nigeria, 200,000 of which took place in the Middle Belt region and the rest as a result of the Boko Haram insurgency in the North-East. About 2.2 million people remained displaced due to these conflicts as of the end of the year. Flooding affected 80 per cent of the country last year and triggered most of the 613,000 new displacements recorded.

PROBLEM For several years, the North-Eastern part of Nigeria has experienced incredible destruction of lives and properties due to the war between the Nigeria government forces and the Boko Haram terrorist group as well as the Fulani herdsmen. As a result of insecurity in the region, a large part of the population has fled from their homes rendering them homeless and almost helpless with little as no hope for a better life. The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees report that there are about 2 million internally displaced persons in Nigeria presently (link to where this data was gotten should be added here for the front-end team to implement so that readers can click on it and it redirects them to where the statement was quoted by the UN) . To address the issue and provide shelter for these people, the Nigerian government has created Internally Displaced Persons (IDP) camps in various parts of the country. However, due to inadequate provisions of basic living ammenities the conditions of people in these camps is quite an eye sore.It is clear there is a severe human rights emergency. Despite the best efforts of the government, people in IDP camps face various harsh conditions such as: • Food insecurity • Social-economic fragility • Chronic poverty • Recurrent health epidemics • Poor Infrastructure • Widespread illiteracy • Limited access to basic living services eg power, shelter etc.


HOW

NairoCare Foundation is a community of like minded young Nigerians passionate about creating positive change in the country and particularly dedicated to help relieve the harsh conditions faced by people living in IDP camps and help accelerate their re-integration back into society. Our mission was born out of the burning desire to solve the biggest problems in the Nigerian society. We intend to leverage the use of technology and humanitarian efforts to alleviate the pain and suffering of internally displaced persons.

Our work:

• We help various IDPs access qualitative health care eg for pregnant women, babies etc • We partner with the industry professionals to help train the IDPs on a current and employable skills which will help them get a decent job in Nigeria which would help ease their re-intergration process. We follow them up by helping them to access funds to start a small scale businesses. • We also ensure that out of school children receive education by helping to pay for school tuition, levies and basic school materials such as books, pen, pencil, and more through donation and direct grants. With our modern techniques and information gathering system, we keep track of IDPs and also keep track of how funds are disbursed using modern technologies. Financial reports of our monthly expenses is published on our website for transparency and proper accountability. We appreciate partners and volunteers who are ready to drive this agenda.
